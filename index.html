Monastic Life | Path of Mindfulness

My journey into mindfulness began in 2016 when I entered a monastery in Bhaktapur. It shaped my values, mindset, and discipline deeply.

Born in Jumla, one of Nepal’s remote regions, I studied in local schools with no access to technology. After the 2015 earthquake, I continued my education as a student monk in Bhaktapur under full scholarship.

I learned Pāli language, moral education, and Pariyatti Shikshya (Buddhist academic curriculum), completing exams up to Level 7–8.

I’ve attended many Vipassana meditation courses — children’s (3-day, 5×), 5–7-day retreats (6×), and 10-day adult courses multiple times at Dhamma Shringa Vipassana Center.

This monastic discipline continues to guide my work in technology — fostering focus, balance, and compassion in innovation.

🕊️ Buddhism as Education: The Path to Awakening

“The mind is everything. What you think, you become.” — The Buddha

Buddhism is not a religion but an education of the mind and heart. The teachings of the Buddha (Dhamma) awaken the inherent wisdom within all beings.

Shakyamuni Buddha was a teacher, guiding people toward virtue, clarity, and freedom through discipline (Sīla), meditation (Samādhi), and wisdom (Paññā).

The Buddha taught that every individual is the owner of their own actions — “Attā hi attano nātho” (the self is its own master). This understanding of karma inspires conscious living.

True freedom arises not by rejecting life but by understanding it — by seeing through desire and ignorance. The simple monastic life embodies this pursuit of peace.

Buddhism teaches ethical living, mindfulness, and compassion — a timeless system of spiritual education and awakening.

Through this practice, one returns to a pure, compassionate, and luminous mind. 🌼
